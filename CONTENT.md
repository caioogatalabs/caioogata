## 2. Brand Voice Guidelines

### Core Principles (Anthropic-Inspired)

**Clarity Above All**
- Simple language, even for complex design concepts
- If using jargon, explain immediately
- Short sentences (maximum 20-25 words)
- One idea per paragraph

**Human and Accessible**
- Write like an intelligent person conversing with another
- Casual but professional, never corporate-speak
- Avoid marketing hyperbole ("game-changing", "revolutionary", "disruptive")
- Active voice: "Led design team" not "Design team was led"

**Honesty and Transparency**
- Don't exaggerate capabilities
- Admit limitations when relevant ("Currently learning AI workflows")
- Use concrete examples over vague claims

**Action-Oriented**
- Clear CTAs
- Benefit before action
- Reduce friction

### Voice Characteristics for Caio

**Professional Athlete Discipline**
- Precise, efficient, no wasted words
- Results-oriented language
- Mentions training, process, iteration

**Technical Literacy**
- Comfortable with dev terminology
- References tools and methods naturally
- Bridges design and engineering

**Philosophical Depth**
- Sports analogies for UX concepts
- References learning through failure
- Influenced by martial arts discipline (Judo)

**YES Examples:**
- "I lead design teams that bridge brand vision and developer experience"
- "Twenty years designing digital products taught me that falling is part of learning"
- "I work best with teams that value iteration over perfection"
- "Based in Porto Alegre, open to remote work with the right opportunity"

**NO Examples:**
- "I'm a passionate, innovative design leader leveraging cutting-edge methodologies" (too much marketing-speak)
- "I'm a rockstar ninja guru" (gimmicky)
- "Obviously, my design systems revolutionize workflows" (arrogant)
- "Simply just click here" (redundant)

### 3.3 Long Bio (300-400 words)

## 10. Philosophy & Personal Story

### 10.1 Design Philosophy Section

**Headline (EN):**
Fall, learn, evolve

**Headline (PT-BR):**
Caia, aprenda, evolua

**Body Copy (EN):**

Great design comes from the same place as great athleticism: the willingness to fail, learn, and iterate.

I learned this first on the Judo mat. As I wrote in 2018: "In Judo, the first lessons are all focused on teaching how to fall. From the beginning, it's understood that you will fall, lose, err. So suffer less, lose the fear, preserve your body and, most importantly, prepare your mind for the obstacles ahead."

Years later, skateboarding taught me the same lesson. Skateboarders are the most persistent people I've ever met. They fall hundreds of times perfecting a single trick, and they do it without fear because falling is part of the process.

This mindset translates directly to design. The best work comes from teams that aren't afraid to test, fail, and iterate. Ship early, get feedback, evolve. Great products aren't born perfect—they're refined through countless small improvements.

As a design leader, I try to create environments where teams can fall safely. Where iteration is celebrated, not feared. Where "I don't know yet" is an acceptable answer, as long as it's followed by curiosity and action.

**Body Copy (PT-BR):**

Ótimo design vem do mesmo lugar que grande performance atlética: a disposição de falhar, aprender e iterar.

Aprendi isso primeiro no tatame de Judô. Como escrevi em 2018: "No Judô, as primeiras lições são todas focadas em ensinar a cair. Desde o início, está entendido que você vai cair, perder, errar. Então sofra menos, perca o medo, preserve seu corpo e, mais importante, prepare sua mente para os obstáculos à frente."

Anos depois, o skate me ensinou a mesma lição. Skatistas são as pessoas mais persistentes que já conheci. Eles caem centenas de vezes aperfeiçoando uma única manobra, e fazem isso sem medo porque cair é parte do processo.

Essa mentalidade se traduz diretamente para design. O melhor trabalho vem de times que não têm medo de testar, falhar e iterar. Lance cedo, receba feedback, evolua. Ótimos produtos não nascem perfeitos—eles são refinados através de incontáveis pequenas melhorias.

Como líder de design, tento criar ambientes onde times podem cair com segurança. Onde iteração é celebrada, não temida. Onde "ainda não sei" é uma resposta aceitável, desde que seguida de curiosidade e ação.

## 14. UX Writing Recommendations

### 14.1 Critical UX Improvements via Copy

**1. Issue: Users may not understand what "Copy as Markdown" means**
   - **Recommendation**: Use tooltip or subtext to explain benefit
   - **Copy**: "Copy as Markdown for AI" + subtext "Paste into Claude or ChatGPT"
   - **Impact**: Increases click-through rate on primary feature by clarifying purpose

**2. Issue: Success feedback after copy may be too generic**
   - **Recommendation**: Contextual success message that guides next action
   - **Copy**: "Content copied! Now open Claude or ChatGPT and paste to get started."
   - **Impact**: Reduces confusion about what to do next, improves completion rate

**3. Issue: Terminal-style headings ($, >) may confuse non-technical users**
   - **Recommendation**: Keep terminal aesthetic but don't let it obscure meaning
   - **Copy**: Use clear section names after terminal prefix: "$ cat experience.txt" where "experience" is human-readable
   - **Impact**: Maintains aesthetic without sacrificing clarity for recruiters

**4. Issue: Footer CTA may be overlooked**
   - **Recommendation**: Slightly different copy than hero to catch attention
   - **Copy**: "Still reading? Copy everything as markdown →"
   - **Impact**: Captures users who scrolled all the way down but didn't click initially

**5. Issue: Mobile clipboard may fail silently**
   - **Recommendation**: Proactive error messaging with solution
   - **Copy**: "Having trouble? Try the Share button below." (on mobile only)
   - **Impact**: Reduces frustration, provides alternative path

## 16. Localization Notes (EN vs PT-BR)

### Cultural Considerations

**Formality Level:**
- **EN**: Slightly more casual, direct, American-influenced tone
- **PT-BR**: Can be slightly more formal in professional contexts, but still approachable
- Both should avoid overly corporate language

**Professional Titles:**
- Keep English titles like "Design Director", "DevEx" even in PT-BR version (industry standard in Brazilian tech)
- Translate descriptions and achievements fully to PT-BR

**Brand Names:**
- Keep brand names in original language (Petrobras, O Boticário, etc.) regardless of interface language
- Do not translate international brand names (Mondelez, Oreo, etc.)

**Sports & Cultural References:**
- Judo, triathlon, skateboarding translate well to both cultures
- Baseball is less common in Brazil, may need brief context in PT-BR: "baseball (três vezes vice-campeão brasileiro nas categorias de base)"
- Video games as design inspiration translates well to both cultures
- Cinema references (Tarantino, Wes Anderson, etc.) are universal

**Tone Differences:**
- **EN**: "I lead teams that ship meaningful work" (very direct, American startup-speak)
- **PT-BR**: "Lidero times que entregam trabalho significativo" (slightly more formal "times" vs "equipes", but still modern)

**Technical Terms:**
- Keep English technical terms even in PT-BR: "design system", "front-end", "DevEx", "PLG"
- Optionally provide brief Portuguese explanation on first mention if helpful

**Measurements & Dates:**
- **EN**: Use MM/YYYY format (e.g., "Oct 2023")
- **PT-BR**: Can use same format or DD/MM/AAAA (e.g., "out 2023")
- Keep training hours in hours/week format for both (10-14h/semana)

## 17. Handoff to Dev Team

### Implementation Notes

**Content Management:**
- All strings should be internationalized (i18n ready)
- Use keys like `hero.tagline.en` and `hero.tagline.pt` for language switching
- Store content in separate JSON or MDX files, not hardcoded in components
- Structure: `/content/en.json` and `/content/pt-br.json`

**Copy-to-Markdown Feature:**
- Generate markdown programmatically from content structure, not by scraping DOM
- This ensures clean, consistent markdown regardless of styling changes
- Use template literals or markdown library (e.g., `remark`, `unified`)
- Include metadata block (YAML frontmatter) at top of exported markdown

**Error Handling:**
- Error messages should be user-facing (never show technical errors directly)
- Always provide actionable next step in error messages
- Example: NOT "Clipboard API failed" → YES "Couldn't copy automatically. Please select the text below and copy manually (Ctrl+C or Cmd+C)."

**Accessibility:**
- Use semantic HTML (H1, H2, H3 hierarchy)
- All interactive elements must have visible focus states
- Terminal aesthetic must maintain WCAG AA contrast ratios (test with contrast checker)
- Provide `aria-labels` for icon buttons and links that open in new tabs

**Mobile Considerations:**
- Copy button must be easily tappable (min 44px touch target)
- Consider Web Share API as alternative on mobile if clipboard fails
- Test on iOS Safari (notorious for clipboard issues) and Android Chrome

**Performance:**
- Lazy load images (if any) below fold
- Minimize JavaScript bundle (don't ship entire markdown library if only using 10%)
- Self-host fonts (JetBrains Mono or Inconsolata) for faster load

**SEO:**
- Meta description should be under 155 characters
- Open Graph image should be 1200×630px
- Page title format: "Caio Ogata - Design Director | Design Systems & DevEx"
- Include structured data (JSON-LD) for Person schema
